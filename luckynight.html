Lucky Night by bis

Music video commission. Developed with TouchDesigner and Blender. Technical examples below (allow videos to load).

Full Video on YouTube

Skinned Animation Textures

This scene shows many varying skinned animations. To achieve this with instancing a python script recorded the bone matrices for each frame of the animation from an FBX. Matrices were then written to a texture and passed as a uniform to a vertex shader.

The bone matrix values could then be read in any order to offset/speed/slow the animation depending on the instance. Several animations could be used efficiently by one shader in one render call.

Character Creation & Animation Workflow

Members of the band 'bis' were scanned using the In3D app to provide an approximation of their likeness for this video. Scans were tidied up in Blender. Finer details, clothing, and accessories were added, including textures developed in Photoshop. Each model was exported with a baked texture atlas for ease of use in TouchDesigner. Models were then auto-rigged with Mixamo and put back into Blender for fine tuning. Using a Mixamo rig later made it very easy to hot swap animations during development. This workflow allowed me to test animations in context to make creative decisions quickly.

Depth of Field & Fake Emissive Lighting

Depth of field is applied as a post process using the depth buffer and a blur. The amount of blur is scaled by the depth of the scene. This allows narrative elements to be brought in and out of focus.

Similarly, the 'Lucky Night' sign is rendered separately, blurred, and composite over to create a glowing effect. A purple light is placed nearby to approximate the illumination the sign would provide to characters and surroundings in lieu of real emissive lighting in TouchDesigner.

Instancing & Video Feedback

This scene made use of video feedback and instancing to create a variety of fun dance scenarios with these characters. The background uses random colours generated by simplex noise. These simple techniques allowed me to build interesting psychedelic scenes quickly.

Procedural Dancefloor & Lights

A procedurally modelled and textured dance floor and ceiling lights complete with static environment mapping for fast reflective illusion. The tiles of the dance floor are driven by a 9x9 pixel noise transformation.

90s Club Environment

The outer shell of the club environment was built in Blender. Interior details were added procedurally including lights, glasses, tables, chairs and bar taps. These were later textured with PBR materials. Cone lights were added outside to simulate those of a city street. Internal lights did not match up to models but instead provided a general atmosphere. A projected texture was used to emulate disco ball reflections.
